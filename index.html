MACRO TRENDS
Pressure for Sustainable materials

In the United States, $35 billion of annual chemical industry sales flow to motor vehicles

A typical automobile that contains more than $3,000 worth of chemistry,

330+ pounds of plastics and polymer composites and 270+ pounds of rubber, textiles, and coatings, and this might increase as vehicle interiors shift from being largely utilitarian to enabling an in-transit experience offering business and entertainment options

More modularity: replacing interior components that fail from increased wear and tear before the rest of the vehicle.

Better Motors

Automobile motors are trying to get smaller, for smaller vehicles. Recent material innovations could drastically improve motor efficiency but the costs are prohibitive; new cooling technology that allows motor size to reduce by 75%. Improved heat removal means motors can carry four times more current than usual to deliver four times more torque.

Electric integration; GM recently declared to go all electric, ideally designing around 20 fully electric models by 2023.

Improved heat removal allows the motor to carry four times more current than usual, and to deliver four times more torque, at a quarter of the size

Additive Manufactoring

Additive manufacturing allows for production of more complex shapes and thus empowers procedural design of parts. Which is key because 'Having the ability to tune the mechanics of materials by simply adjusting its geometry opens the door to a wide variety of practical applications...including strong lightweight structural materials for airplanes, cars, buildings' - MIT Researcher

Procedurally generated form takes traditional parts and optimizes it for strength and minimal material. Additive processes enables the mass manufacture of such complex shapes, which is key to the innovation in certain materials (see below)

Started being used for making super lightweight aerospace parts
